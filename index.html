About Me

Hi, I'm Dhanuka Perera, a Lecturer (Probationary) at the Department of Entrepreneurship, University of Sri Jayewardenepura. My mission is to guide individuals in unlocking their full potential, promoting self-reliance, and fostering personal growth through support and encouragement. I hold an MSc in Management and have extensive experience in teaching and research related to entrepreneurship, ICT adoption, and SME performance.

Research & Publications

Entrepreneurial Bricolage in SMEs

Exploring the interplay of divergent thinking, self-efficacy, and gender dynamics. Awarded Best Paper in Entrepreneurial Leadership at ICBM 2024.

Technology Organization Environment Framework

An empirical investigation on ICT adoption of SMEs in Sri Lanka, published in the Journal of Integrated Marketing Communications and Digital Marketing.

Information and Communication Technology Adoption Models

Published in the Sri Lanka Journal of Entrepreneurship, this work provides a literature review on ICT adoption models in SMEs. (Volume 2, Issue 2, pp 10-28)

Relationship between the Antecedents of ICT Adoption and Business Performance

Published in the Sri Lankan Journal of Entrepreneurship, exploring the relationship between ICT adoption and business performance in SMEs. (Volume 2, Issue 1, pp 39-48)

Teaching & Courses

Corporate Entrepreneurship

A course designed to introduce students to the dynamics of entrepreneurial activities within established organizations.

Fundamentals in Business Planning

Helping students develop comprehensive business plans, preparing them for the real-world challenges of entrepreneurship.

Introduction to Entrepreneurship

Introducing students to fundamental concepts in entrepreneurship, taught at the Faculty of Technology, USJ.

Economics for Entrepreneurs

Covering economic concepts that are critical for entrepreneurs, part of the Diploma in Entrepreneurial Business Management at University of Sri Jayewardenepura.

Projects & Contributions

Heta Lowata Diriyak CSR Program

Coordinated the Heta Lowata Diriyak CSR Program, which aimed to empower underprivileged communities through entrepreneurship initiatives.

SECRA Project

Participated in the SECRA Project funded by Erasmus+, contributing to strengthening university-enterprise collaboration in Asia. Presented WP5 in Thailand and Sweden conferences, and was an organizing committee member for various project events.

Market Survey Project - Ministry of Trade

Successfully completed a market survey analyzing the impact of the price stabilization strategy of 27 essential goods for the Ministry of Trade, Commerce and Food Security in Sri Lanka.
